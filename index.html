Alcatraz — Know Before You Go

⏰ Timing

Be at Pier 33 by 1:30 PM — at least 30 min before departure to check in, clear security and board. The ferry leaves promptly and does not wait for late arrivals.

Ferry crossing ~15 min. Plan ~2–2.5 hours on the island; return ferries run regularly and you can take any of them — no evening deadline.

👕 What to wear

Layers — the bay and island are windy and 10°F+ cooler than downtown, even in July. Fleece or windbreaker recommended.

Comfortable walking shoes — the dock-to-cellhouse walk is a steep ¼-mile uphill (an accessibility tram is available).

🎒 Security & bags

All bags are subject to search. Max one standard backpack 16" × 20" per person.

No luggage, coolers, picnic baskets or oversized bags — there is no storage at Pier 33 or on the island, and you'll be denied boarding (refunded) if you bring them.

🚫 Allowed / not allowed

Not allowed: weapons, bikes, skateboards, scooters, skates, wheelie shoes, drones. No smoking or pets. Pro camera gear needs NPS authorisation.

Allowed: bottled water anywhere on the island; snacks — but eaten only at the dock picnic area. Only water is sold on the island, so eat lunch before boarding.

Trip Tips

  • Dress in layers. Mornings 55–60°F, afternoons 60–70°F, windier and cooler at the coast and on the bridge. Fleece + windbreaker + comfortable shoes.
  • Download offline maps for San Francisco and Marin — service is spotty at Muir Woods, Mt. Tam and the Headlands.
  • Print the Muir Woods voucher before Sunday. It must be shown on arrival and there's no connectivity at the park.
  • Fill the gas tank in Marin before driving back Sunday — easier and cheaper than downtown.
  • Pack a daypack Sunday: water, snacks, sunscreen, portable charger, camera.
  • Let yourselves linger. The plan has slack — an extra 20–30 minutes under the redwoods or at Battery Spencer is what you'll remember.
